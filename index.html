A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

( 15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015 )

Biography

  • Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015.
  • Abdul Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the development of India's missile and space programs. He played a key role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile systems.
  • Kalam began his career as a scientist at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and later joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He made significant contributions to the development of India's guided missile program, particularly the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Beyond his scientific achievements, Abdul Kalam was widely respected for his simplicity, humility, and inspirational leadership. He was a popular figure among the youth and often spoke about the importance of education, science, and technology in nation-building. Kalam was known for his strong vision of a developed India and his efforts to ignite the minds of young people.
  • After his presidency, Abdul Kalam continued to be actively involved in various educational and humanitarian initiatives. He received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.
  • Abdul Kalam's contributions to science, technology, and education, along with his humble and inspiring personality, have made him one of the most beloved figures in India and an inspiration to people around the world.
